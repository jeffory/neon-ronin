**Verdict: fail**

### Reference Match
There is a total thematic mismatch between the reference (rainy cyberpunk night) and the game (sunset skyscrapers). Even ignoring the theme, the game fails to match the reference's *intent* for density, depth, and material complexity; the game implementation is extremely primitive, consisting of simple textured cubes floating in a void.

### Goal Assessment
While the stated elements are technically present (two rooftops, a bridge, a helipad decal, and a sunset skybox), the implementation is placeholder-tier. The interior is extremely sparse, the "AC units" are untextured primitive cubes, and the skyscrapers lack any base or environmental integration, failing the requirement for "correct level geometry."

### Issues

#### Issue 1: Floating Scene Geometry
- **Type:** logical inconsistency
- **Severity:** major
- **Frames:** 1, 2
- **Location:** bottom of skyscrapers
- **Description:** The skyscrapers are floating in the middle of the skybox with no base, fog, or lower-city geometry to ground them, making the level feel like a test map rather than a cohesive environment.

#### Issue 2: Primitive/Placeholder Assets
- **Type:** placeholder
- **Severity:** major
- **Frames:** 1, 3, 4, 6
- **Location:** rooftop and interior
- **Description:** The AC units (Frames 1, 3) are simple dark cubes, the bridge is a basic slab with no structural detail, and the interior furniture (Frame 6) consists of primitive shapes that lack the fidelity expected of a finished level.

#### Issue 3: Poor Interior Lighting & Composition
- **Type:** visual bug
- **Severity:** major
- **Frame:** 6
- **Location:** entire room
- **Description:** The interior office is extremely flat and dark, lacking proper light bounces or shadows. The furniture is placed arbitrarily in a vast, empty room with no consideration for layout or realistic scale.

#### Issue 4: Texture Tiling and Scaling
- **Type:** style mismatch
- **Severity:** minor
- **Frames:** 1, 2
- **Location:** skyscraper facades
- **Description:** The glass/window textures on the skyscrapers are highly repetitive with visible tiling seams, creating a "checkerboard" effect that looks unnatural.

#### Issue 5: Helipad Implementation
- **Type:** visual bug
- **Severity:** note
- **Frames:** 2, 4
- **Location:** rooftop center
- **Description:** The helipad is a flat, low-resolution decal that doesn't interact with the concrete texture beneath it, appearing to "float" on the surface rather than being painted on it.

### Summary

The level is a primitive block-out that lacks the environmental density and lighting quality required for a finished skyscraper scene, with the floating buildings and placeholder geometry being the most critical failures.
